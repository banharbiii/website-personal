My Kind of TV Shows

Action & Superhero

1. The Boys

Genre: Action, Dark Comedy, Superhero | Creator: Eric Kripke | Seasons: 3 (4th coming 2024)

Plot Summary:

The Boys is set in a universe where superheroes are owned by a powerful corporation and often abuse their powers. A group of vigilantes known as "The Boys" set out to take down corrupt superheroes with no more than their will to fight.

Main Cast:

Karl Urban

Jack Quaid

Antony Starr

Erin Moriarty

Awards & Achievements:

  • Critics' Choice Super Award for Best Superhero Series
  • Saturn Award for Best Streaming Superhero Television Series
  • Primetime Emmy Award nominations
  • One of Prime Video's most-watched original series

Why I Love It:

The Boys is a brutal deconstruction of superhero mythology, showing what would really happen if superheroes existed in our world. It's violent, hilarious, and surprisingly thoughtful about celebrity culture and corporate power. Homelander might be the most terrifying villain on TV right now.

Notable Episode:

"Herogasm" (S3E6) - The most outrageous superhero orgy scene ever filmed, mixed with genuine emotional stakes.

2. Echo

Genre: Crime Drama, Superhero | Creator: Marion Dayre | Seasons: 1 (Limited Series)

Plot Summary:

Maya Lopez must face her past, reconnect with her Native American roots, and embrace her family and community if she ever hopes to move forward following her violent actions in New York City.

Main Cast:

Alaqua Cox

Vincent D'Onofrio

Charlie Cox

Cultural Impact:

  • Marvel's first TV-MA rated series
  • Significant representation of deaf and Native American communities
  • Much of the dialogue in ASL and Choctaw language
  • Grounded, street-level approach to the MCU

Why I Love It:

As Marvel's first TV-MA series, Echo brings a grounded, street-level intensity to the MCU. Maya Lopez's journey as a deaf Native American antihero is compelling, and the return of Kingpin is always a treat. The action scenes are some of Marvel's best.
